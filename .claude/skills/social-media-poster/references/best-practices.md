## Hashtag Strategies

### Facebook
- **Optimal Count**: 1-2 hashtags
- **Max Count**: 2 (more reduces engagement)
- **Strategy**: Use only highly relevant, branded hashtags
- **Placement**: End of post with line break

### Instagram
- **Optimal Count**: 20-30 hashtags
- **Max Count**: 30 (hard limit)
- **Strategy**: Mix of popular (100K+ posts) and niche (10K-50K posts)
- **Placement**: After main content with double line break
- **Categories to Mix**:
  - 5-10 popular hashtags (#Business, #Entrepreneur, #Innovation)
  - 10-15 niche hashtags (industry-specific)
  - 5-10 branded hashtags (your company/campaign)

### Twitter
- **Optimal Count**: 1-2 hashtags
- **Max Count**: 2 (more reduces engagement)
- **Strategy**: Use trending or highly relevant hashtags only
- **Placement**: Inline with content or at end

### LinkedIn
- **Optimal Count**: 3-5 hashtags
- **Max Count**: 10 (hard limit, but 3-5 recommended)
- **Strategy**: Use professional, industry-specific hashtags
- **Placement**: End of post with line break
- **Focus**: B2B, professional development, industry trends

## Optimal Posting Times

### Facebook
- **Best Days**: Wednesday, Thursday, Friday
- **Weekday Times**: 1:00 PM - 3:00 PM (peak engagement)
- **Weekend Times**: 12:00 PM - 1:00 PM
- **Frequency**: 1-2 posts per day
- **Avoid**: Early morning (before 9 AM), late night (after 10 PM)

### Instagram
- **Best Days**: Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday
- **Weekday Times**: 11:00 AM - 1:00 PM (peak engagement)
- **Weekend Times**: 10:00 AM - 11:00 AM
- **Frequency**: 1-2 feed posts per day, 3-5 stories per day
- **Avoid**: Late night (after 11 PM)

### Twitter
- **Best Days**: Wednesday, Friday
- **Weekday Times**: 8:00-10:00 AM and 6:00-9:00 PM (peak engagement)
- **Weekend Times**: 9:00-10:00 AM
- **Frequency**: 3-5 tweets per day
- **Avoid**: 3:00-5:00 PM (low engagement)

### LinkedIn
- **Best Days**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- **Weekday Times**: 7:00-9:00 AM and 12:00-2:00 PM (peak engagement)
- **Weekend Times**: Avoid weekends (low B2B engagement)
- **Frequency**: 1 post per day (max 5 per week for optimal engagement)
- **Avoid**: Weekends, early morning (before 7 AM), late evening (after 6 PM)

## Content Formatting Guidelines

### Facebook

**Length**:
- Optimal: 40-80 characters (highest engagement)
- Max: 63,206 characters (technical limit)
- Sweet spot: 1-2 sentences

**Format**:
```
[Main message - keep short and punchy]

[Optional: Additional context]

[Call-to-action question]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2
```

**Emojis**: Use moderately (1-2 per post)

**Links**: Include link in post (preview will show automatically)

**Best Practices**:
- Use videos and images (5x more engagement than text-only)
- Ask questions to encourage comments
- Keep it conversational and authentic
- Use line breaks for readability

### Instagram

**Length**:
- Optimal: First 125 characters (shown before "more" button)
- Max: 2,200 characters
- Hook: First line must grab attention

**Format**:
```
[Hook - first 125 characters are CRITICAL]

[Main content - tell the story]

[Call-to-action]

[Double line break]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3 ... (20-30 hashtags)
```

**Emojis**: Use liberally (3-5 per post)

**Links**: NOT clickable in captions (use link in bio)

**Best Practices**:
- First 125 characters are most important (shown before "more")
- Use high-quality square images (1080x1080px)
- Post reels for 2x more reach
- Use location tags and tag relevant accounts
- Engage with followers' content daily
- Use all 30 hashtags (doesn't look spammy on Instagram)

### Twitter

**Length**:
- Optimal: 240 characters (leaves room for retweets with comments)
- Max: 280 characters
- Sweet spot: 200-250 characters

**Format**:
```
[Main message - be concise and punchy]

[Optional: Additional context]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2
```

**Emojis**: Use moderately (1-2 per tweet)

**Links**: Automatically shortened (count as 23 characters)

**Best Practices**:
- Images get 150% more retweets
- Create threads for longer content (use create_thread tool)
- Ask questions and create polls
- Use 1-2 hashtags maximum
- Tweet 3-5 times per day
- Engage with replies quickly

### LinkedIn

**Length**:
- Optimal: 150-300 characters (highest engagement)
- Max: 3,000 characters
- Sweet spot: 1-2 paragraphs with clear value proposition

**Format**:
```
[Hook - professional and value-driven]

[Main content - insights, expertise, or story]

[Call-to-action - professional engagement]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3
```

**Emojis**: Use sparingly (0-1 per post, professional context only)

**Links**: Clickable and show preview (use link_url parameter in MCP)

**Best Practices**:
- Focus on professional value and insights
- Share industry expertise and thought leadership
- Use data and statistics to support claims
- Tag relevant companies and professionals
- Engage with comments professionally
- Post during business hours (avoid weekends)
- Use professional tone (avoid casual slang)
- Include clear takeaways or actionable insights

## Engagement Optimization Tips

### Facebook
- **Videos**: Get 5x more engagement than text-only posts
- **Questions**: Posts with questions get 100% more comments
- **Response Time**: Reply to comments within 1 hour for best results
- **Post Type**: Native videos > Photos > Links > Text
- **Engagement**: Like and reply to comments to boost reach

### Instagram
- **Reels**: Get 2x more reach than regular posts
- **First 125 Characters**: Most important for engagement
- **Hashtags**: Use all 30 (mix popular and niche)
- **Location Tags**: Increase discoverability by 79%
- **Tagging Accounts**: Tag relevant accounts for cross-promotion
- **Stories**: Post 3-5 per day for consistent presence
- **Engagement**: Engage with followers' content daily (like, comment)

### Twitter
- **Images**: Get 150% more retweets than text-only
- **Threads**: Use for longer content (better than single long tweet)
- **Polls**: Get 3x more engagement than regular tweets
- **Hashtags**: 1-2 maximum (more reduces engagement)
- **Timing**: Tweet during peak hours (8-10 AM, 6-9 PM)
- **Engagement**: Retweet and reply to relevant content

### LinkedIn
- **Professional Content**: Thought leadership posts get 3x more engagement
- **First 150 Characters**: Most important for engagement (shown in feed preview)
- **Hashtags**: Use 3-5 professional hashtags (more reduces reach)
- **Native Content**: Posts without external links get 2x more engagement
- **Timing**: Post during business hours (7-9 AM, 12-2 PM)
- **Engagement**: Comment on industry posts and engage with connections
- **Long-Form**: Articles and longer posts (1000+ words) perform well
- **Data & Insights**: Posts with statistics get 37% more engagement

## Image Upload Instructions

### Facebook
```python
# Use create_post with image_path
create_post(
    message="Your content here",
    image_path="/path/to/image.jpg",
    link="https://example.com"  # Optional
)

# Or use dedicated upload_photo
upload_photo(
    image_path="/path/to/image.jpg",
    caption="Your caption here"
)
```

### Instagram
```python
# Use create_media_post (image required)
create_media_post(
    image_path="/path/to/image.jpg",
    caption="Your caption with hashtags"
)

# For stories
create_story(
    image_path="/path/to/image.jpg"
)

# For reels
create_reel(
    video_path="/path/to/video.mp4",
    caption="Your caption"
)
```

### Twitter
```python
# Use create_tweet with media_paths
create_tweet(
    text="Your tweet here",
    media_paths=["/path/to/image.jpg"]
)

# Or upload media first, then attach
media_id = upload_media("/path/to/image.jpg")
create_tweet(text="Your tweet", media_ids=[media_id])
```

### LinkedIn
```python
# Use create_post with link_url (images via URL, not local path)
create_post(
    text="Your post content here",
    hashtags=["Innovation", "Business", "Tech"],
    link_url="https://example.com/article",  # Optional
    link_title="Article Title",  # Optional
    link_description="Article description"  # Optional
)

# Note: LinkedIn API requires images to be hosted at public URLs
# For image posts, upload to CDN/S3 first, then use link_url

## Quality Scoring Criteria

When creating content, aim for these scores:

**90-100 (Excellent)**: Ready to post
- Optimal length for platform
- Appropriate hashtag count
- Includes call-to-action
- Has emojis (if appropriate)
- Image meets specifications

**80-89 (Good)**: Minor improvements suggested
- Length slightly off optimal
- Could use 1-2 more hashtags
- CTA could be stronger

**70-79 (Fair)**: Several improvements needed
- Length too long or too short
- Missing hashtags or too many
- No call-to-action
- Image issues

**<70 (Poor)**: Significant improvements required
- Major length issues
- No hashtags
- No engagement elements
- Image doesn't meet specs

## Content Creation Workflow

1. **Analyze Request**: Understand what user wants to post
2. **Create Base Content**: Write compelling message
3. **Optimize for Each Platform**:
   - Facebook: Keep short (40-80 chars), add 1-2 hashtags
   - Instagram: Strong hook (first 125 chars), add 20-30 hashtags
   - Twitter: Concise (240 chars), add 1-2 hashtags, create thread if needed
4. **Add Engagement Elements**: CTA, emojis, questions
5. **Validate Image** (if provided): Check dimensions and file size
6. **Check Timing**: Suggest optimal posting time if not optimal
7. **Create Approval Requests**: One per platform with optimized content
8. **Execute via MCP**: After approval, post to platforms
